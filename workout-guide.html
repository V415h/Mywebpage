Workout Guide: Best Exercises for Each Muscle Group

Chest

  • Bench Press - Classic compound movement for overall chest mass and strength.
    How to: Keep your feet flat, back arched, and lower the bar to mid-chest. Press up explosively.
    Safety: Always use a spotter or safety bars. Keep wrists straight and avoid bouncing the bar off your chest.
  • Incline Dumbbell Press - Targets upper chest and shoulders.
    How to: Set bench to 30-45째. Lower dumbbells to chest, press up and together.
    Safety: Use a weight you can control. Keep shoulders retracted and avoid overextending elbows.
  • Push Ups - Great bodyweight exercise for chest and triceps.
    How to: Hands shoulder-width, body straight, lower until chest nearly touches floor, push up.
    Safety: Keep core tight, avoid sagging hips or flared elbows.

Back

  • Pull Ups - Builds width and strength in the upper back and lats.
    How to: Use a full range of motion, pulling chin above the bar.
    Safety: Avoid swinging or kipping. Use a grip that feels comfortable for your shoulders.
  • Barbell Row - Excellent for mid-back thickness and overall strength.
    How to: Hinge at hips, keep back flat, pull bar to lower ribs.
    Safety: Do not round your back. Start light and focus on form.
  • Lat Pulldown - Good alternative to pull ups for all levels.
    How to: Pull bar to upper chest, squeeze lats, control the return.
    Safety: Do not lean back excessively. Use a moderate weight for control.

Legs

  • Squat - The king of leg exercises for quads, glutes, and core.
    How to: Chest up, knees out, squat to at least parallel.
    Safety: Keep heels down, do not let knees cave in, use a spotter for heavy sets.
  • Leg Press - Allows heavy loading for leg growth.
    How to: Feet shoulder-width, lower sled until knees are at 90째, press up.
    Safety: Do not lock out knees. Keep lower back pressed into the seat.
  • Lunges - Improves balance and hits all leg muscles.
    How to: Step forward, lower until both knees at 90째, push back.
    Safety: Keep front knee over ankle, torso upright, and use controlled steps.

Shoulders

  • Overhead Press - Builds overall shoulder mass and strength.
    How to: Stand tall, press bar overhead, lock out elbows.
    Safety: Do not arch lower back. Keep core braced and wrists straight.
  • Lateral Raise - Isolates the side delts for width.
    How to: Raise dumbbells to shoulder height, slight bend in elbows.
    Safety: Use light weights, avoid swinging, and control the movement.
  • Face Pull - Great for rear delts and shoulder health.
    How to: Pull rope to face, elbows high, squeeze rear delts.
    Safety: Use a light weight, keep shoulders down and avoid jerking.

Arms

  • Bicep Curl - Best for biceps peak and size.
    How to: Elbows still, curl weight up, squeeze at the top.
    Safety: Avoid swinging or using your back. Lower the weight slowly.
  • Tricep Pushdown - Isolates the triceps for strength and definition.
    How to: Elbows at sides, push bar down, extend fully.
    Safety: Do not flare elbows. Use a weight you can control.
  • Dips - Compound movement for triceps and chest.
    How to: Lower body until elbows at 90째, press up.
    Safety: Keep shoulders down, avoid going too deep, and use assistance if needed.

Core

  • Plank - Builds core stability and endurance.
    How to: Body in a straight line, squeeze glutes, hold position.
    Safety: Do not let hips sag or pike. Breathe steadily.
  • Hanging Leg Raise - Targets lower abs and hip flexors.
    How to: Hang from bar, raise legs to 90째, control down.
    Safety: Avoid swinging. Use slow, controlled reps.
  • Russian Twist - Works obliques and rotational strength.
    How to: Sit, lean back, twist torso side to side.
    Safety: Keep back straight, move slowly, and avoid rounding lower back.

Cardio & Conditioning

  • Running - Improves cardiovascular health and endurance.
    How to: Use a comfortable pace, land mid-foot, keep posture upright.
    Safety: Wear proper shoes, avoid overtraining, and stay hydrated.
  • Swimming - Full-body, low-impact cardio.
    How to: Use smooth strokes, focus on breathing and technique.
    Safety: Swim in supervised areas, warm up before, and avoid swimming alone.
  • Rowing Machine - Great for full-body conditioning and stamina.
    How to: Push with legs, pull with arms, keep back straight.
    Safety: Do not round your back, use a controlled pace, and adjust resistance as needed.
